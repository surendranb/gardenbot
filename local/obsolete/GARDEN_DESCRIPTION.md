# 🌿 GardenOS: Desk-Top Environment Description

## The Setup
- **Location**: Indoor table-top (Chennai, India context).
- **Automation**: **NONE**. There is no automated watering, no capillary system, and no timed irrigation.
- **Watering Method**: Fully manual. The human (Surendran) waters based on system recommendations or personal observation.
- **Plants**:
    1. **String of Nickels (p1)**: Small vines in a yellow pot. Known for resistive sensor noise.
    2. **Mexican Mint (p2)**: Fuzzy succulent in a black pot. Shares space with Pilea.
    3. **Pothos (p3)**: Standard variegated vine in a black pot. Uses the "White Rabbit" anchor.
    4. **Silver Guest (p4)**: Pilea cluster living in the same pot as the Mexican Mint (p2).

## The Philosophy: "Local Truth over Sensor Data"
- **Sensor Vulnerability**: Sensors are susceptible to air pockets, soil aeration, and hardware drift.
- **Visual Superiority**: If a sensor reports 5% moisture (DRY) but the leaves are turgid and plump in `latest.jpg`, the sensor is assumed to be **MALFUNCTIONING**.
- **Human Override**: The system provides "Intelligence," but the Human provides the "Final Audit." Reports must highlight these discrepancies so the human can decide whether to ignore a "Dry" alert.
- **Weather Sensitivity**: Even though they are indoors, the 30°C+ Chennai heat and low humidity significantly impact their manual watering schedule.

## 1. Executive Summary
The garden biome currently exhibits a state of "stasis-stress." While the String of Nickels (P1) maintains high vitality, the Pothos (P3) and the Mexican Mint (P2/P4) cluster are showing signs of growth stagnation and minor localized necrosis. The discrepancy between sensor data and visual evidence—specifically regarding the Pothos (P3) soil moisture—indicates that environmental stressors (high VPD, air scouring) are overriding raw hydration levels.

## 2. Evidence Correlation & Audit

This suggests that while soil moisture is technically present, the atmospheric demand (VPD) is causing transpiration stress faster than the root system can recover, especially given the air-scouring from fans.
- **Recent Interventions:** The manual misting (04/05) has not yet manifested in improved vegetative progression.

## 3. Botanical Recommendations (Next Steps)
1. **Address Soil Compaction:** The visual audit notes surface crusting on P2/P4. Gently aerate the top 5mm of substrate to improve water infiltration and oxygen access for the seedlings.
2. **Hydration Strategy:** Ignore the P3 sensor (78.6%) as ground truth. Given the leaf-tip necrosis, perform a targeted light watering of the P3 root zone, prioritizing localized humidity over total saturation.
3. **VPD Mitigation:** The high fan usage is likely causing excessive transpiration. Consider repositioning P3 further away from the direct line of Fan S or limiting fan duration during peak solar-thermal hours (12:00-15:00).
4. **Seedling Support:** P2/P4 requires a more stable environment. If possible, provide a small transparent humidity dome or cover to aid P4's establishment and reduce moisture loss from the P2/P4 cluster.
5. **Observation:** Continue monitoring the P3 leaf necrosis to see if the rate of degradation slows following minor adjustments.
